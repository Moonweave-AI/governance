# Moonweave Governance Semantic Core: Foundations

## Non-negotiable Principles

1. **Asset cleanliness and provenance traceability**: Code, data, models, media, prompts, firmware, and binaries must have provenance, license, version or hash, Owner, permitted use, and restrictions.
2. **Global observability and behavioral traceability**: Key inputs, state, versions, tool calls, outputs, and evaluation records must be sufficient for audit, localization, or reasonable replay.
3. **Embodied convergence and fail-safe**: Physical execution authority must be separately authorized; it is bounded by default, graded authorization, supervisable, abortable, and fail-safe.
4. **State persistence and implementation replaceability**: Models, frameworks, languages, and services are tools; long-term state, semantic continuity, and evolution history must not be locked in by any temporary implementation.
5. **Unified domain contract, distributed implementation**: Authoritative concepts, schemas, protocols, state, and compatibility strategies are shared across repositories, front/back ends, Agents, Infra, and embodied boundaries; internal implementations may differ due to domain constraints.
6. **Build for lifespan, quality built into every change**: Experiments, shared components, and the production baseline use different thresholds; routine changes must not unexplainedly degrade overall health.
7. **Transmissible systems over hero engineering**: Critical knowledge, permissions, maintenance responsibilities, and decisions must be inheritable by others.
8. **Transparent by default, open when possible**: Open when safety, privacy, law, IP, and third-party obligations permit; public assets must have license, provenance, status, and maintenance responsibility.

## Conflict Resolution

Legal/safety/privacy/ethics bottom line → Mission alignment → Real impact → Minimal blast radius and reversibility → DRI ruling → RFC/ADR or Issue/PR trail.
